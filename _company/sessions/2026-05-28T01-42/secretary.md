# 📱 영숙 — 회사 목표, 모든 에이전트의 개인 목표 파일(_agents/{id}/goal.md), 그리고 최근 메모리 데이터를 취합하고, 현재 가장 높은 우선순위와 시급성을 가진 '단일 최적 작업'을 결정하여 브리핑할 것.

Based on the totality of the discussions, goals, and established strategic frameworks, the project has successfully moved from the **Ideation/Discovery Phase** to the **Strategic Definition Phase**.

The current highest-leverage point, and therefore the single most critical next step, is to bridge the gap between *theoretical strategy* and *real-world validation*. We must transition from asking "What should we build?" to answering **"Who will pay for it, and how will they use it?"**

***

### 🎯 The Single Highest Priority Action:
**Develop and Execute a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) Validation Plan.**

### 💡 Rationale for this Priority:
Currently, we have strong conceptual models, defined target demographics, and potential feature sets. However, the primary risk remaining is **Product-Market Fit (PMF)**. Without validating the core assumptions with real users and real budget constraints, any further development is speculative.

The MVP Validation Plan forces the team to:
1.  **De-scope:** Ruthlessly cut features down to the absolute core value proposition.
2.  **Define Metrics:** Establish clear, measurable success metrics *before* building anything (e.g., sign-up rate, time spent on core feature, willingness to pay).
3.  **Test Quickly:** Focus on the fastest, cheapest way to get feedback (e.g., landing page mockups, concierge service, limited beta access) rather than building a full-scale product.

### 📋 Required Deliverable for This Step:
A structured document containing the following three sections:

**1. Core Hypothesis Statement (The "Why"):**
*   *Format:* "We believe that [Target User Group] will achieve [Desired Outcome] by using [Our Core Feature] because [Underlying Insight]."
*   *Output:* A single, testable, and measurable statement.

**2. MVP Scope Definition (The "What"):**
*   A list of **3-5 absolute essential features** that deliver the core hypothesis.
*   A list of **3-5 "Future Scope" features** that must be explicitly deferred until PMF is proven.

**3. Validation Plan (The "How"):**
*   **Target Users:** A defined persona for the first 10-20 test users.
*   **Test Method:** The specific, low-cost method of validation (e.g., 5-person user interviews, a dedicated landing page with a "Waitlist/Pre-order" button, a concierge beta test).
*   **Success Criteria:** The quantitative metrics that will signal that the hypothesis is validated (e.g., "If 4 out of 5 interviewees state they would pay $X for this solution, we proceed to Build Phase 1").

***

**Conclusion:** By focusing on the MVP Validation Plan, we transform our existing strategic documents into a concrete, actionable, and risk-mitigating roadmap. This is the single step that maximizes our chances of achieving product-market fit efficiently.
